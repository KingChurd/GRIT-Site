Pillar Three

Spiritual GRIT

Identity, purpose, and connection. Spiritual GRIT is about who you’re becoming underneath the noise — in your faith, your values, and your daily decisions.

Identity Purpose Integrity

Also see: Physical GRIT · Mental GRIT

What Spiritual GRIT Is About

Spiritual GRIT is not about having all the answers. It’s about staying anchored to your deepest values while you wrestle with questions, change, and real life.

  • Clarifying who you are and who you’re becoming
  • Aligning your choices with your stated values
  • Giving your strength a direction, not just intensity

Core Habits of Spiritual GRIT

Regular Reflection

Taking time to step back from the noise and ask bigger questions about your life and direction.

  • “Who am I becoming right now?”
  • “Is this choice aligned with my values?”
  • “What kind of man do I want to be in this season?”

Practicing Your Values

Turning beliefs into behaviors. Not perfect, but intentional steps that match what you say matters most.

  • Choosing honesty when hiding would be easier
  • Showing up for people you’ve committed to
  • Letting your faith or principles touch your calendar

Wrestling Honestly

Making room for doubt, questions, and growth without throwing away your integrity in the process.

  • Admitting when things don’t make sense
  • Seeking wise counsel instead of just online noise
  • Letting struggle deepen, not destroy, your character

What This Looks Like in Real Life

In Faith Transitions

You don’t fake certainty or burn everything down. You keep showing up to your values, even while you ask hard questions and re-evaluate beliefs.

In Relationships

You work to be the same man in private that you say you are in public — even when nobody’s watching and it would be easier not to.

In Daily Decisions

You make small, quiet choices that line up with who you want to become, instead of chasing every impulse or short-term escape.
