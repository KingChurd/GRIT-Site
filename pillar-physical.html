Pillar One

Physical GRIT

Strength, movement, and recovery – built for real life, not a fantasy schedule. The goal isn’t to become a full-time athlete. It’s to build a body that can actually carry your life.

What Physical GRIT Is About

Physical GRIT isn’t about chasing a perfect physique. It’s about building a body that lets you show up for your people, your work, and your purpose — without burning out or breaking down.

  • Having enough energy to be present after work
  • Feeling capable and strong instead of fragile and fried
  • Using movement to support your mind and mood

Core Habits of Physical GRIT

Move Most Days

Not perfect workouts — consistent movement. Walks, short sessions, or simple circuits that your real life can handle.

  • 7–15 minute routines you can repeat
  • Walk breaks instead of endless scrolling
  • “Some” instead of “all or nothing”

Build Load-Bearing Strength

Focus on basic patterns: push, pull, hinge, squat, carry. Enough strength to pick things up, play with your kids, and feel solid.

  • Bodyweight work to start
  • Simple progressive overload over time
  • Form and control over ego lifting

Protect Recovery

You can’t out-discipline trash recovery. Sleep, breathing, and simple decompression are part of the program, not a reward.

  • Basic sleep rhythm (even if imperfect)
  • Short wind-down rituals instead of crashing
  • Learning to step down from “always on” mode

What This Looks Like in Real Life

Busy Dad Scenario

10-minute bodyweight session before the kids wake up, a walk at lunch 3 days a week, and shutting screens down 20 minutes earlier at night.

Student + Work Scenario

Walks between classes, 2–3 short strength sessions per week, and one intentional “off-screen” evening for real recovery instead of doomscrolling.

Burned Out & Restarting

Start with walks and gentle movement only. Let your nervous system calm down first, then slowly reintroduce more intense training.
