Pillar Two

Mental GRIT

Thoughts, emotions, and habits of mind. Mental GRIT is not about never struggling — it’s about learning how to respond instead of just react.

Awareness Resilience Clarity

Also see: Physical GRIT · Spiritual GRIT

What Mental GRIT Is About

Mental GRIT means you’re honest about what’s going on inside, but you don’t let it drive the car. You learn to notice, name, and navigate your thoughts and emotions with more skill instead of white-knuckling everything or shutting down.

  • Understanding your stress and triggers
  • Building better self-talk and internal scripts
  • Creating space between what you feel and what you choose

Core Habits of Mental GRIT

Daily Check-Ins

Short, honest check-ins instead of pretending you’re fine until you snap or shut down.

  • “What am I feeling right now?”
  • “Where do I feel it in my body?”
  • “What do I actually need?”

Grounding & Regulation

Practical tools for bringing your nervous system down a notch so you can think clearly again.

  • Breathing and grounding exercises
  • Time-boxed worry or journaling sessions
  • Stepping away before reacting when possible

Reframing & Story Work

Learning to challenge the unhelpful stories in your head and write better ones, without lying to yourself.

  • Noticing “always/never” thinking
  • Replacing shame scripts with accurate ones
  • Seeing hard seasons as chapters, not the whole book

What This Looks Like in Real Life

Before a Hard Conversation

Take 5 minutes to breathe, name your fears, and decide what you actually want from the conversation instead of going in hot or completely guarded.

On Overloaded Mornings

When you wake up heavy, you don’t just push it down. You do a quick check-in, pick one priority, and give yourself permission for the day to be “lighter but still moving.”

When Shame Shows Up

Instead of “I’m a failure,” you learn to say, “I messed that up, but I’m still a man who cares and can try again,” and then choose the next small right action.
