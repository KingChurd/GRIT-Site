Resources for Support

GRIT is one piece of the puzzle. Here are other tools, professionals, and crisis resources you can lean on as you build strength.

Crisis & Immediate Help (U.S.)

If you are in immediate danger or thinking about hurting yourself, please do not wait on an app or website. Reach out now:

  • Call or text 988 – Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
  • Call 911 or your local emergency number
  • Go to the nearest emergency room or urgent care

Therapy & Professional Support

  • Use the GRIT therapist finder to explore options that fit your situation.
  • Search for local therapists who specialize in men’s mental health, trauma, or addiction.
  • Look for group therapy or support groups if you need community as well as a therapist.

Helpful Practices

  • Short daily walks, even if you don’t “work out” that day.
  • 5-minute grounding or prayer practice before bed or after work.
  • Writing down one win, one struggle, and one intention each day.

Recommended Learning

As GRIT grows, this section will include specific books, podcasts, and trainings that align with GRIT’s values.

  • Foundations of habit building and behavior change.
  • Healthy masculinity, relationships, and emotional regulation.
  • Faith, purpose, and identity work that supports real life.
