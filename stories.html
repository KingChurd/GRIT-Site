GRIT Stories

These are composite stories based on patterns we see in real men – not exact individuals. As GRIT grows, this page will include real member stories (with their permission).

“I was fried, not lazy.”

35, married, kids, full-time work + school

He thought he was just unmotivated. In reality, he was running on fumes – work, kids, school, and no recovery. GRIT helped him start with 7-minute movement sessions and a basic sleep reset. No six-pack, no magic. Just enough momentum to feel like a man who could carry his load again.

“I didn’t know how to say what I felt.”

28, engaged, wrestling with anger and shutdown

He wasn’t trying to be distant. He just didn’t have language for what was going on inside. Grounding exercises, therapist support, and GRIT reflections gave him a way to name his stress before it exploded or went silent. His relationships didn’t suddenly become perfect, but they became honest.

“Faith, doubt, and still showing up.”

40, long-time believer, now questioning

He didn’t want to lose his faith or his integrity. GRIT’s spiritual pillar helped him keep showing up to his values while wrestling honestly with his questions. No one told him what to believe. The focus was on who he was becoming in the middle of the wrestle.
